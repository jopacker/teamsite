If you’re guessing, you can have it wrong

We aim to understand our users first by doing research, before actually designing and building something, which might otherwise be based on guesses and which often turns out to be wrong. This process is called user-centred design.

Design Research

We use user centred design and ethnographic tools and techniques to uncover behaviours, latent needs and motivations that guide us in designing meaningful products and services.

Digital products

Our products are developed by collaborative teams of strategists, engineers and designers. They identify product opportunities, develop product concepts then prototype and test them with users.

Service Design

Many university services are fragmented into series of loosely controlled moments. We will deliver those we work on based on context, and personalise the entire experience to each customer.
